Key Learnings
& 2027 Recommendations

What worked, what didn't, and what to change for next year. Drawn from both the paid campaign (Dec 2025–Apr 2026) and organic event performance (Jun 2026).

Paid Campaign Learnings
✓ What Worked
Carousel format consistently outperformed every other ad type on CTR and conversions — make it the default for 2027
Testimonial content (real community voices) drove the highest CTR (4.73%) — social proof beats branded messaging every time
Sector-specific copy (Medtech, Biotech, named companies) outperformed generic health messaging — personalisation at scale works
Feb CPM was extremely efficient (€0.04/click) — the algorithm rewarded high-relevance creative
✗ What to Avoid
GIF format — €115 CPM, 0 conversions. Never use again on LinkedIn
Video-only ad sets without a paired CTA unit — 26K+ views but 0.15% CTR. Awareness without action is wasted spend
52% of budget on Video Views objective — algorithm was never optimised for actual conversions. Mis-allocation from the start
Only €6 on Website Conversions objective — next year this should receive a significant budget share if lead generation is the goal
Organic Event Learnings
✓ What Drove the Spike
Real-time, human content from the floor (behind-the-scenes, live session snapshots) outperformed polished content significantly
Active reposting from the PFMD page of attendee content amplified reach into new networks beyond existing followers
Award moment posts (Sneha Dave, Jury Special Award) drove emotional engagement and shares from outside the usual audience
Post-event content (Jun 5–6) still generated 6,665 impressions — the reflection wave is real and worth planning for
△ Gaps to Address
No UTM tracking on organic posts — impossible to know how much LinkedIn traffic converted to registrations or site visits
Conversion path from organic LinkedIn to actual attendee/sponsor is still unclear — needs a proper attribution setup for 2027
2-month gap between paid stopping (Apr) and event (Jun) — any audience warm-up effect likely dissipated
PFMD page data only — attendee posts and other org pages not captured, meaning actual PEOF LinkedIn footprint was significantly larger
Recommendations for PEOF 2027
Paid Strategy
Shift budget mix: 50% carousel (conversion-optimised), 30% video + paired CTA, 20% retargeting warm audiences
Run paid until 2–3 weeks before the event, not 2 months before
Zero budget on Video Views objective alone — always pair with a conversion unit
ABM targeting (named accounts) + testimonial creative = highest ROI combination
Organic Content
Pre-assign live content roles: 1 person per session for real-time posts, 1 person coordinating reposts from the page
Plan a 3-day post-event content calendar to capture the reflection wave (currently leaving reach on the table)
Brief all speakers and award nominees on LinkedIn visibility — the Sneha Dave post proved personal stories amplify far beyond the PFMD audience
Measurement
Add UTM parameters to all LinkedIn posts linking to the PEOF or PFMD site — without this, the conversion path is invisible
Set up a LinkedIn Insight Tag on the PEOF/PFMD website to track organic LinkedIn visitors post-event
Track new follower quality (not just count) — 112 new followers in the right industries is worth more than 500 irrelevant ones
For the paid campaign, define conversion actions clearly upfront (registration, contact form, sponsorship enquiry) before the first euro is spent

Post-Boost Analysis & Learnings
✓ What Worked
CTR improved over the campaign duration — from 0.087% on Day 1 to 0.314% on Day 7. The algorithm iteratively found better-performing audience pockets. A longer flight or higher budget could have capitalised further on this upward trend
Jun 10–11 were the sweet spot — highest impressions (3,927) and highest clicks (11 each), at the cheapest CPC (€7.26–€7.27). Day 3 and 4 of the flight consistently outperform in brand awareness campaigns as the algorithm stabilises
Pharma and life sciences audience was correctly reached — AstraZeneca, IQVIA, Medtronic, Thermo Fisher, Pfizer, BMS, Novo Nordisk all in the top 10. Exactly the right companies for a patient engagement message
Senior-level penetration was strong — 61.4% of impressions at Senior grade, with 78.6% at Senior, Manager or Director. Decision-maker exposure was high for a brand awareness flight
Switzerland over-indexed on CTR — 0.70% CTR with 8 clicks despite being the 5th market by impressions. Swiss pharma cluster (Basel, Zürich) is likely very relevant to the PEOF audience and responded strongly
△ Gaps and Limitations
Zero conversions tracked — the campaign ran with Brand Awareness (CPM) objective and no conversion tracking was attached. We know 23 people clicked through to the landing page but have no data on what they did next
US and UK were the two largest impression markets but among the weakest click performers — 0.157% CTR for UK, 0.098% for US. These may be colder audiences less familiar with PEOF. Spain and Switzerland outperformed significantly despite fewer impressions
Organic vs paid engagement gap is stark — the same post drove 147 organic clicks (9.5% ER) from ~1,554 impressions, vs 47 paid clicks (0.21% CTR) from 21,967 impressions. This is expected (cold vs warm audiences) but underscores that paid reach is quantity-driven, not quality-driven at this stage
Flight length tapered out — Jun 13–14 spend dropped to €52–57/day vs the €76–82 average, suggesting budget pressure in the final days. CTR was actually rising during these days, meaning the algorithm had found optimal pockets but the budget was thinning
No retargeting layer — this was a single cold-reach campaign. A follow-up retargeting ad to the 47 clickers and engaged users would have been a natural next step at very low incremental cost

PEOF 2026 Post Boost — Final Verdict: The boost successfully extended the wrap-up post to 21,967 additional professionals, including senior-level staff at AstraZeneca, IQVIA, Medtronic, Thermo Fisher, and 20+ other key pharma and health institutions. Switzerland, Spain, and Madrid metro areas showed the strongest click engagement. The campaign delivered below-benchmark CPM (€22.76 vs typical €25–35 for this sector) and improving CTR over the flight. For a post-event brand awareness push with zero conversion infrastructure needed, it was well-executed. The recommendation for 2027 is to either pair a similar boost with a LinkedIn Insight Tag retargeting pool, or to flip the objective to Website Visits if a registration or sponsor page needs driving.
